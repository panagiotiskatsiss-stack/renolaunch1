#2: Price-Shoppers & "Just Give Me a Number" Homeowners

Most contractors have worked with marketing that technically produces leads but in practice creates headaches.

  • Homeowners who only want a "ballpark" price, then go silent the moment real numbers are discussed
  • People calling 35 contractors at once with no urgency or intention to decide anytime soon
  • Appointments booked purely to "get a quote," not because they're ready to move forward
  • Hours wasted on estimates for jobs that never had a real chance of closing

It's like trying to climb a mountain. You're asking someone who doesn't know you, doesn't trust you, and didn't plan on replacing their roof this week... to commit tens of thousands of dollars on one of the biggest purchases they'll ever make often with zero education, zero context, and zero follow-up.

#3: You're The Bottleneck In The Business

Even if demand is coming in and leads are solid... growth still feels capped because everything runs through you.

  • You're managing everything yourself. Marketing, follow-ups, booking, estimates it all funnels back to you.
  • You can't step away without revenue slowing down.
  • You're working 60+ hours a week without proportional growth.
  • You can't delegate because there are no real systems in place.
